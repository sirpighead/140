Freshman Year

Jack McLaughlin

The inside look into my freshman year of college.

First Semester: Part 1

I'll start off by saying that I don't think I could ask for a better first semester of college. I had a lot of fun, enjoyed my classes(mostly), and met an amazing group of friends. The year began, obviously, with freshman orientation. I was placed in team 5-L, and I had no idea how lucky I was. It was on this team that I met my core group of friends, a friend group which has, since then, simultaneously grown bigger and closer together (Shout out to Richard Yu, my orientation leader, for fostering the social environment that sparked all of our friendships). We had a team chant, team wave, and lots of team spirit. These guys, and the rest of my friends who later joined us, made not only my first semester, but also my entire college experience so far, amazing.

The first semester was very busy, which is good because it made it go by quickly. I spent a lot of time hanging out with friends, but I also joined the sailing club and spent most of my weekends on the water. I had never sailed before, so I didn't really know what to expect; I didn't know if the team was competitive, or if I was going to be a good fit. My worries were unfounded, though, and the people in the club were super welcoming, and gave me a great introduction to one of my new favorite hobbies. The best practices were the ones where, instead of doing races or drills, we just explored the bay and blasted music.

First Semester: Part 2

I had a crazy first couple months at RIT, and although it slowed down as the end of the semester approached, I still went on plenty of adventures with my friends. While it was still warm out, we made sure to go to Durand Eastman Beach for late nights by the lake. We never did end up seeing it during the day. In October, my friend Will proposed we all go to a local state park, Letchworth. We packed into two cars, picked up sandwiches, and set out for the drive. This time, too, we got there as the sun was going down. It seemed to be a theme for us. Nevertheless, we still had fun and the views were amazing. We even had matching frog hats.

Having local friends - and friends with cars - was a godsend. Will, and another friend Amanda, lived in nearby towns and invited us to hang out at their houses pretty often. Having somewhere to go off campus was super refreshing, and we all loved meeting their adorable pets. Of course, we needed transportation, so luckily my friends Braelyn, Carey, and (again) Will had their cars. We tried our best to fit everyone in when we had somewhere to go, but often some unlucky souls got stuck with the trunk. We only went short distances with trunkers, like to our friends houses, the beach, or out to restaurants like China Buffet. Around the holidays, we threw mini-birthday parties for our friends, and even got invited over to Will's house for Friendsgiving. While everyone started heading home for winter break, our friend Sylvie taught a bunch of us the basics of crocheting, and we made cute dinosaurs and bumble bees. It was a great way to end a great semester.

Second Semester

The second semester of my first year has gone by in the blink of an eye, seemingly way faster than the first. After winter break, it definitely took a few weeks to get back into the swing of things. The weather was bleak and cold, and we were all focused on our new classes, and we went on less adventures. So when my friend Charlie told me about a concert in Syracuse, I was super excited to have a reason to hang out off campus. I had my car this semester, so I drove and it was an amazing set. I went to two more concerts back home, later in February. I saw Cordae with my friends, and then Billie Eilish with my family, which were both in Boston.

I hung out a lot more on-campus this semester. My friends and I would meet up for ice cream late at night and hang out in the lounges in the Sol or Fish dorms, or even just the Ellingson laundry room. Everyone always had projects or homework to do, so this also became group studying times. Once it got nicer out (above 30 degrees because the bar is on the floor in Rochester), we spent a lot of time outside enjoying what little sun we had. There were also a lot of Spring birthdays, so we basically had a little birthday party every week since we all got back from Spring break. These turned into weekly movie nights, and we decided to finish the Twilight Saga by the end of the semester The finale, Twilight Breaking Dawn Part 2, we are actually watching during my birthday party. The end of the semester has been crazy busy, so it is nice to have down time with friends.
